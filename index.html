Today, I want to share with you my experience with an exchange blood transfusion.
It's a special procedure that helps people like me with sickle cell anaemia.
This is the machine that helps exchange old sickle-shaped red blood cells for fresh, healthy ones.
Hello! My name is Chimkariwe Obuseh, but you can call me Chimka.
I have sickle cell anaemia, which means my red blood cells can sometimes change shape, get stuck and cause pain. Today, I'm here to show you how exchange blood transfusions help make me feel better.
This is a cannula. It's a small plastic tube that the doctors place into your vein.
It's not scary at all, and it connects you to the exchange blood transfusion machine.
Instead of using two cannulae, I have a special device called a double lumen port-a-cath.
It's like a hidden treasure chest under my skin, and it allows the doctors to easily exchange my blood without needing multiple tubes.
Here I am, ready for my exchange blood transfusion! A parent should come with you for the procedure. I brought my mum, but my dad brings me sometimes :).
That's me lying next to the blood exchange machine! The machine knows exactly how much blood to exchange.
During the transfusion, I'm connected to the machine through my port-a-cath.
It takes out my old blood and replaces it with fresh, healthy blood.
It's important to drink a lot of water during the transfusion, or you'll become very tired!
